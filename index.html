In the background you see an implementation of the boids program, simulating the flocking behavior of animals such as birds or fish.

This program is an example of a very simple self-organized system. The movements of the boids are not decided by a central controller, but locally by the boids themselves.

In a confined space, the boids will eventually converge towards a single, large swarm. Since it would be a little boring, the model here is modified slightly for the boids to spread out if they start to feel too much cramped.

If you want to see them better, you can click the button right next to this one to bring the demo to the foreground (you can click it again to bring it back in the background).
